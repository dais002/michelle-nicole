I always thought the conventional way to life was to go to college, pick a major, and stick to one career for the rest of your life. At 21, freshly graduated out of college, do you really have your career all figured out? I thought I did.

As a little girl, I had so many interests and passions. In High School, I was first exposed to the language of html when Angelfire websites were a “thing”, following the blogging era with Xanga and Blogger, which I loved stylizing. In college, my major was Computer Science for a semester until I realized learning Java put me to sleep, so I quickly switched and finished with a Business degree with a concentration in Finance.

Starting my career at a financial software company, I worked my way up the corporate ladder within 4 years. I solved client issues everyday with my communication and analytical skills as a Client Services Representative, continued learning by taking over 20+ internal certifications to become Senior, then built empathy to form my role as a Team Lead, where I loved mentoring others and leading my team to succeed. My career took a turn when I made one of the hardest decisions in my life at the time to quit my job and follow my heart pursuing my passion in photography. This was where my empathy & passion for all things LOVE was expressed and my self-motivation, grit, and hustle of entrepreneurship was born. My last role in Business Development for 3 years made me learn first hand the gravity of creating human-centric designs and products around your customers first.

“...the gravity of creating human-centric designs and products around your customers first.”

“...blending both my business and creative side together to help discover, adapt, evolve, and grow.”

I’ve accepted it’s natural to evolve to look for a greater meaning to life. I have a lot of goals and ideas I want to contribute and accomplish in my lifetime, and UX/UI design blends both my business and creative side together to help discover, adapt, evolve, and grow.

When I’m not busy “working”, my energy is expended towards health & wellness, food, wine, photography, and connecting with others over a good coffee &/or espresso. I have an innate love to help and connect with others, to help others first before my own needs, and do good in this world. I aspire to reach some of my life goals which include humanitarian & philanthropy work.

I also have a mission to make an impact so our next female generation becomes strong, confident, positive, and influential women.

I’d love to connect and have a great conversation over coffee!
